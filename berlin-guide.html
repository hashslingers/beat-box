The NYC → Berlin translator

Recalibrate your instincts before you land.

Your New York reflexes are mostly wrong here. A conversion table so you stop making the obvious newcomer mistakes in week one.

In New York…In Berlin…
Tap-to-pay everythingCash is still king. Spätis, many bars, some clubs and even doctors are card-shy. Carry euros.
BodegaThe Späti — corner shop + social club. Buy a beer, they crack it open, you drink it on the curb. That's a night.
Broker fee + bidding warRent is ~40–50% under London/Paris, but you'll apply to 20–40 flats to land one viewing.
SSN / credit scoreSCHUFA (credit) + Anmeldung (address registration). You arrive with neither.
24/7 citySundays are closed. Supermarkets shut. Spätis, bakeries and brunch save you.
Craigslist / eBayKleinanzeigen — where the city buys & sells everything, guitars included (see §08).
Tip 20% or you're a monsterRound up ~5–10%, said out loud as you pay. But tip bar & door staff in cash — thin margins.

Where to actually live

Pick a Kiez the way you'd pick between Bushwick and the LES.

The real question is where a New Yorker on a normal salary should aim in 2026, and where the energy leaks next. Investropa 2025

The value play

Wedding

"Still has room to breathe." New cafés, galleries, co-working, cheaper rent. Sprengelkiez near Nordufer is the pocket.

NYC ≈ Bed-Stuy before everyone agreed it was Bed-Stuy.
The frontier

Lichtenberg · Alt-Treptow · Moabit

Rents climbing 15–20%/yr — the tell the artists already arrived. Get in before the listicles.

NYC ≈ Ridgewood / deep Bushwick.
The classic

Neukölln (Schillerkiez)

Still the engine room of cool, grittier edge, brunch-and-bars density, with Tempelhofer Feld on your doorstep.

NYC ≈ The LES: "over" for a decade, still where it happens.
Family-quiet

Rixdorf · Friedenau

Rixdorf: an 18th-c. cobblestoned village inside Neukölln. Friedenau: leafy, calm, near Zig Zag jazz club — a real family pick.

NYC ≈ The pocket of the West Village that forgets it's in a city.

If it's all over your feed, the locals already left. Aim one Kiez further out.

The bureaucracy move-kit

The part that ambushes every American. Work it like a project.

Germany runs on a chicken-and-egg loop: you need an address to register (Anmeldung), registration to get a bank account and credit file (SCHUFA), and often a SCHUFA to rent the address. The way through: land in a furnished short-term flat / WG that lets you register, get the Anmeldung, build a SCHUFA, then hunt the real place from inside the city. Living in Germany 2026

Your first-90-days checklist
Why your blank SCHUFA is the whole problem
Your credit file starts empty, and many landlords want a SCHUFA before they'll rent. Furnished platforms skip it — which is exactly why you start there, register, open a bank account, season a couple of months of paid rent, then compete for unfurnished. Income rule: net pay ≥ 3× monthly rent (≈€2,900 net → ~€900–950). HousingAnywhere

Live jazz & music

For a guitarist, this is the real reason to move. Curated rooms + the live calendar up top.

Berlin is a genuine jazz capital with a room for every night of the week. Pair these venues with the live Jazzity programme in §01 to see who's actually on tonight. And block out Jazzwoche Berlin 2026 (Jun 20–26) — 30+ clubs, jams, a free opening at Gretchen. IG Jazz Berlin

Charlottenburg institution named for Coltrane; Marsalis, Hancock, Mehldau, Diana Krall have all played it. Late-night Saturday jam is the move. visitBerlin

With the family

A decommissioned airport turned giant open field: bikes, kites, skating, BBQ, community gardens. The city's best free Sunday with kids.

08

The jazz-guitar corner

Germany's "eBay for guitars," plus the great minds behind the archtops.

Good news for a guitarist: Germany is archtop country, and the secondhand market is deep. The live searches sit in §01; here's how to read the scene and who the names are.

The marketplace

Kleinanzeigen

The German eBay-classifieds (ex-eBay Kleinanzeigen) — local, cash, collection-in-person. Search "Jazzgitarre" / "Archtop", save the search, enable the alert. This is where deals actually live.

NYC ≈ Craigslist + FB Marketplace, but everyone uses it.
Curated & shipped

Reverb & Vintage&Rare

For graded vintage with photos and return policies. Berlin storefronts like Vintage Guitars Berlin and Golden Age Guitars regularly list German archtops here.

NYC ≈ Reverb is the same Reverb — just filter region to Germany.
The great minds

German archtop luthiers

The heritage to hunt: Artur Lang, Roger (Wenzel Rossmeisl), Höfner, Framus, Klira — the Bubenreuth / Markneukirchen lutherie tradition. Hand-built jazz boxes at a fraction of a Gibson.

NYC ≈ Your D'Angelico/D'Aquisto lineage — but local and findable.
The brain trust

Forums & communities

For ID, valuation & setup wisdom: the JazzGuitar.be forum, German Gitarre & Bass and Bonedo. Post a Kleinanzeigen link there before you buy.

NYC ≈ The Gear Page, but with German archtop obsessives.
How to actually buy off Kleinanzeigen without getting burned
Meet in person, bring cash, play it unplugged first (archtop = acoustic tells you everything), check the neck angle and binding, and lowball politely — "Ist der Preis verhandelbar?" goes a long way. For anything shipped, insist on PayPal Goods & Services, never a bank transfer to a stranger. Reverb listings: Vintage Guitars Berlin.
09

Sundays & secondhand

The shops are shut. The locals are at the Flohmarkt.

Furnishing a flat from scratch? The Sunday flea market is your IKEA-avoidance plan and your social life at once. visitBerlin

  • Arkonaplatz — mid-century furniture, GDR-era design, the connoisseur's pick for outfitting a flat.
  • Boxhagener Platz (Friedrichshain, Sun 10–6) — cheaper than Mauerpark; vinyl, books, furniture.
  • Mauerpark — touristy in the centre, but the outer ring and the open-air Bearpit Karaoke still earn it.
  • Kranoldplatz (Neukölln) & the new-format Leopoldplatz market (since Jan 2026). Travel2Berlin
10

The unspoken rules

Do these and you'll read as a local, not a transplant.
  • Pfand belongs beside the bin, not in it. Bottles carry a deposit; leave them out for collectors.
  • Cross on green. Berliners wait at empty red pedestrian lights. Jaywalking marks you instantly.
  • Quiet hours are real. Sundays + after 22:00, noise rules are enforced by neighbours. Respect the Hausordnung — doubly so with a kid's drum kit.
  • Cash, and say the tip out loud when you pay; don't leave coins and bolt.
  • Learn ten words of German anyway. Tschüss, Genau, Alles gut buy grace even though everyone speaks English.

Use this as the current state of play — then go disappear into your own Kiez.
